Post harvest losses constitute a significant 20% chunk of the global agricultural
produce. The number goes up to 40% for fruits and vegetables. The main reason
for the losses are the inefficient cold storage capacities with the farmers.
The reason behind farmers storing their produce is the fluctuation in the pricing
anticipating a higher price in future. The root cause for the fluctuations are the
presence multiple middlemen present in the supply chain increasing the supply chain
cost with negligible value addition. Both the ends of supply chain farmers and processors
face losses with farmer bearing the maximum impact. Startups in the ecosystem provide a
marketplace to facilitate farmer-processor trade but with the regulation in price that
space is also occupied by middlemen.


Nutshell provides a SaaS based platform enabling direct trade between farmer and processor.
The model is similar to Uber where the processor(Passenger) enter their demand(destination)
and the software provides the nearest farmer(driver) who can satisfy the order(ride). Apart
from this just like Uber has a fixed cost which varies only on the distance we at Nutshell also
have our own platform price varying only on the crop traded. The price we fix on the platform
remains constant for a month removing the worries about fluctuation in prices. This has been
achieved by deployed deep learning models and trained them across various datasets and taking
the forecasted price from them and finally performing statistical modelling to level it for a
period of one month. We are the only company which deal with regulating the prices other than
the government's MSP. Our revenue comes from two ways, One as a monthly subscription fee for
the farmers to list their produce on the platform and another a per order commission from
the processor side based on the order quantity. Our final goal is to establish a perfect
farm2fork supply chain and connecting processors has been our first step to achieve our vision.
